Off-duty Hong Kong firefighter turns himself in over viral shopping centre row

SCMP | FREE | 337 words | 2026-07-13

An off-duty Hong Kong firefighter has been arrested after surrendering to police over an alleged assault, the South China Morning Post has learned, following a viral video showing him shouting at and shoving a young couple in a Mong Kok shopping centre.

A source said on Monday that the 48-year-old man turned himself in at Mong Kok Police Station on Sunday, accompanied by his wife. He told police he was a firefighter.

“The arrestee admitted that he had acted that way as he suspected the [other] man … had intentionally bumped into him,” the source said.

The arrest followed an online backlash triggered by a 24-second video, posted on Instagram on Saturday, showing an altercation between a middle-aged pair and a younger couple at Sim City shopping centre in Mong Kok.

In the video, the older couple accuse the younger pair of bumping into them at a crowded escalator entrance.

The video begins with the middle-aged woman saying she did not squeeze herself into the younger couple.

The middle-aged man, dressed in a striped green T-shirt and wearing a green cap, then shouts at the young couple at close quarters, accusing them of bumping into him and his partner.

The young woman screams in fear as the middle-aged man presses on with his accusation, pointing at the young man and later shoving him.

The middle-aged woman is also heard demanding an apology from the young couple. The young man subsequently apologises, but the middle-aged couple shout at them to leave.

“[You said] sorry? Go away! B***** off!” the middle-aged man yells after the young man apologises.

The Instagram clip attracted more than 1,000 comments over the weekend, with many internet users criticising the middle-aged man for being rude.

The incident also provoked a heated discussion on social media platform Threads, with some users disclosing the man’s occupation as a firefighter on Sunday evening.

The source said the Mong Kok district crime investigation team was overseeing the case.
