Your Hong Kong weekend drinks guide for July 9-11

SCMP | FREE | 490 words | 2026-07-13

It’s new menus for this weekend as 67 on Hollywood unveils its latest wine wall with a selection of otherwise members-only pours; and Orchard updates its menu with all-new eau de vie offerings showcasing a wide range of fruit spirits and brandies. Meanwhile, at Coffeelin’s Fortress Hill location, you will find a string of new signatures and later opening hours.

Thursday, July 9

67 on Hollywood presents Pages from the Black Book

Ease into the weekend with 67 on Hollywood’s recently updated wine wall. The concept by wine-centric private members’ club 67 Pall Mall is giving Hong Kong’s oenophiles a rare, limited-time sneak peek into the club’s members-only Black Book collection of wines. The wall features 67 bottles across four chapters: Terroir & Tension, Depth & Opulence, Silk & Precision, and Power & Conviction.

The first two comprise white wines: the 2022 Sancerre La Côte, Gerard Boulay, has a precise minerality and represents the Loire Valley terroir, while the 2023 Domaine Muxin Chardonnay hails from Yunnan and brings complexity from the region’s high altitude.

The latter two categories are represented by red wines. The 2022 Antonio Quari, Ladoix, Les Briquottes, Cuvée Lou is a pinot noir brimming with notes of fresh berries and silky tannins, while the 1998 Château Figeac blends cabernet sauvignon, cabernet franc and merlot equally to produce dark fruit and cedar notes.

Where: 41 Hollywood Road, Central

When: 4pm-12am, Tuesday to Saturday, until August 8

Friday, July 10

Orchard updates its menu

Hong Kong’s house of eau de vie – or fruit brandy – recently overhauled its menu with new signatures that show off the many forms fruit spirits can take. Orchard’s menu is divided into four categories, three of which received new libations. The Peach-zza features apple vodka, Parmesan, peach cordial, tomato water, basil and pink peppercorn in the Umami-Fruit category. In the Spirit-Led category, you will find drinks like the William’s Martini, focusing on the Poire Williams pear eau de vie, and the Floral Fusion with jasmine umeshu alongside peach baijiu, London dry gin, lychee, dry vermouth and cardamom.

For something more fruit-forward, try Toasted Tropic from the Playful category, built around pisco with sesame, falernum, pineapple, orange blossom water, cream and citrus.

Where: 28 Gage Street, Central

When: 6pm-1am on Sunday to Wednesday; 6pm-2am on Thursday to Saturday

Saturday, July 11

Coffeelin’s new cocktail menu

If you’ve been looking for cocktails to enjoy in Fortress Hill, Coffeelin has you covered as it works with Rafael Arevalo (formerly of Club Bâtard) to present six signature cocktails at its flagship location, as the cafe extends its hours to 11pm from Thursday to Saturday. The drinks range from refreshing and classic to strong and sophisticated. The Somewhere Over Fortress Hill combines passion fruit with whisky and coriander, whereas the Tequila Made Me Do It builds on tequila with oat milk and coconut water.

Where: 228 Electric Road, Fortress Hill

When: 5pm-11pm
