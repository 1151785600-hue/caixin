2 arrested over viral video row involving off-duty Hong Kong firefighter

SCMP | FREE DEEP | 557 words | 2026-07-15

An off-duty Hong Kong firefighter has been arrested after reporting an alleged assault as a victim, the South China Morning Post has learned, following a viral video showing him shouting at and shoving a young couple in a shopping centre.

A source said on Monday evening that a second man, 33, had been arrested in connection with the same case and was suspected of having bumped into the firefighter’s wife at the Sim City shopping centre in Mong Kok on Saturday. He was detained on suspicion of common assault.

“Police arrested another man after investigating and suspecting that he had a run-in with the partner earlier in the incident, causing the dispute and the altercation afterwards,” the source said, after the firefighter was arrested earlier in the day.

Police confirmed earlier that a 48-year-old man was arrested on Sunday in connection with a dispute involving his wife and another couple in the mall the previous day.

“The arrestee is suspected to have used his hands to push another man,” a police spokesman said.

The Fire Services Department confirmed that the man was one of its employees.

The firefighter’s arrest followed an online backlash triggered by a 24-second video, posted on Instagram on Saturday, showing an altercation between a middle-aged pair and a younger couple at Sim City shopping centre.

In the video, the older couple accuse the younger pair of bumping into them at a crowded escalator entrance.

The video begins with the middle-aged woman saying she did not squeeze herself into the younger couple. However, it is unclear whether the dispute began at the start of the video or beforehand.

The middle-aged man, dressed in a striped green T-shirt and wearing a green cap, then shouts at the young couple at close quarters, accusing them of bumping into him and his partner.

The young woman screams in fear as the middle-aged man presses on with his accusation, pointing at the young man and later shoving him.

The middle-aged woman is also heard demanding an apology from the young couple. The young man subsequently apologises, but the middle-aged couple shout at them to leave.

“[You said] sorry? Go away! B***** off!” the middle-aged man yells after the young man apologises.

However, a department source said the dispute was believed to have started when the off-duty firefighter suspected a man had brushed against his wife while they were inside the mall.

A department spokesman said: “The staff member accompanied his wife to report a case at a police station, explaining what happened and providing information for the investigation.”

A separate source said the 48-year-old and his wife went to Mong Kok Police Station to report a suspected assault on her.

The firefighter was subsequently arrested on suspicion of common assault.

“The arrestee admitted that he had acted that way as he suspected the [other] man … had intentionally bumped into him,” the source said.

The Instagram clip attracted more than 1,000 comments over the weekend, with many internet users criticising the middle-aged man for being rude.

The incident also provoked heated discussion on social media platform Threads, with some users disclosing the man’s occupation as a firefighter on Sunday evening.

The force said the Mong Kok district crime investigation team was overseeing the case.
