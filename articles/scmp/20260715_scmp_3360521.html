Hong Kong woman gets 10 years in jail for luring victims into gang’s extortion plot

SCMP | FREE DEEP | 618 words | 2026-07-15

A Hong Kong woman has been sentenced to 10 years in prison for assisting in robbing three people when she was a teenager by luring them to a deserted location before her fellow gang members attacked them and took nude photos for extortion.

The High Court sentenced 23-year-old Hau Yan-ki on Tuesday after a jury rejected her assertion that she had mistakenly believed she was just helping her friends to collect debts from the victims.

She was among six people – two women and four men – arrested in connection with a series of extortion schemes perpetrated by the gang in November 2020.

Hau, who was 17 at the time, met her targets on social media and dating apps and persuaded them to take a taxi with her to an isolated location in Yuen Long.

The five victims – four men and a woman – were beaten up and forced to transfer money to her accomplices before they were released.

The female victim, identified in court only as X, was forced to eat grass, undress and masturbate in front of a camera, before another unidentified male victim was forced to perform oral sex on her.

The gang obtained HK$107,900 (US$13,760) in cash, three mobile phones and six bank cards from victims Chan Wai-yan and Fung Ka-ho, who had to call their relatives for ransom and had nude photos taken of them before being released.

The fifth victim also lost his wallet, an iPhone worth HK$10,000 and a bank card, which the gang subsequently used to withdraw HK$3,000.

A seven-member jury unanimously convicted Hau of two counts of robbery and one of attempted robbery, while acquitting her of a second count of attempted robbery, after a two-week trial in May.

In mitigation, Hau’s defence counsel highlighted her complicated family background and said she went astray after associating with other youngsters at a children’s home.

The court heard the defendant committed the offences at the request of her former same-sex partner, whom she met while serving time at a juvenile facility for shoplifting in 2016.

Just before her arrest, Hau became pregnant after developing a romantic relationship with a man and gave birth to her son, now aged four, while in custody.

Lawyers said Hau had completed two nutrition courses to equip herself with practical skills for the future, as she was determined not to let her son repeat her past mistakes.

Mr Justice William Tam Yiu-ho said Hau was part of a joint enterprise to rob the victims even though she did not assault or extort them.

“In my assessment, while she was clearly not the mastermind, she played an important role for these crimes to be perpetrated on the victims,” the judge said.

He jailed the defendant for seven years on each count and ordered a partially consecutive sentence, for a total of 10 years.

The court also sentenced two men to prison on Tuesday for their roles in the crimes.

Taxi driver Mak Kim-man, 65, received 4½ years in prison after the jury cleared him of robbery but found him guilty on the alternative charge of blackmail.

The jury found that Mak could have honestly believed he was only assisting in debt collection when he drove Fung and Hau to Yuen Long on November 22, 2020.

Lifeguard Lau Ka-yu, 27, was jailed for seven years after pleading guilty to a count of robbery, two of attempted robbery and two of criminal intimidation in relation to robberies targeting Fung, X and the unidentified male victim.

Three other defendants – Chan Yat-hang and Leung Chun-wa, both 26, and 27-year-old Cheung Yuk-sim – were previously jailed for between 22 months and 9½ years on charges including robbery, blackmail, criminal intimidation and money laundering.
