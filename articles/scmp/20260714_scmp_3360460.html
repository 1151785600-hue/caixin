Mark Wahlberg savours Penang’s charm as Malaysians floored by star’s humility

SCMP | FREE DEEP | 540 words | 2026-07-14

Mark Wahlberg shed the aura of a Hollywood star when he savoured street food and mingled with locals in Penang while filming Netflix’s The Big Fix. His trip delighted Malaysians and highlighted the country’s growing appeal to global productions.

Videos of Wahlberg, who played a former Boston police detective in Spenser Confidential, filming around the Malaysian state went viral on social media over the weekend.

Some clips showed the 55-year-old walking through a market, waving to onlookers and visiting a mamak outlet, one of the country’s ubiquitous Indian Muslim food stalls.

Fans were also impressed with the actor’s down-to-earth presence.

“He is sitting at the mamak stall that casually? Malaysians can claim him already,” a social media user said.

Others struggled to believe that an A-list actor had turned up so casually in George Town, Penang’s capital.

“I hate that we live in a time that makes us question if everything is AI,” a commenter said.

Another offered Wahlberg some local dining guidance. “Dear Mr Wahlberg. We Malaysians use [a] spoon to eat rice.”

Shopkeepers were urged to put up banners declaring “Mark Wahlberg was here”, while fans proposed a full Penang food itinerary of nasi kandar, char kway teow and roti canai before he leaves the northern island.

One fan wondered whether Wahlberg still woke up at 3am for the gym while in Penang, and another asked, “Where is Ted?” in a nod to his foul-mouthed teddy bear comedy.

Southeast Asia’s blockbuster traffic has more often flowed through Thailand, where an attractive rebate scheme has drawn 100 foreign productions since 2017, including The White Lotus and Jurassic World Rebirth.

Malaysia is also attracting international studios by offering a 30 per cent incentive on filming costs.

Penang Island Mayor A Rajendran told local media that the filming for The Big Fix directed by Iceland’s Baltasar Kormakur began on Friday at locations including Lorong Kulit, City Stadium, Batu Kawan Stadium and Macallum Field.

The crime thriller, co-starring British actor Riz Ahmed, follows a former Interpol officer working inside Fifa who uncovers a global match-fixing network and pursues a well-connected agent tied to organised crime.

The cast also includes Singaporean actor Chin Han, Indian actor Vipin Sharma and US-based Malaysian actress Nuha Jes Izman.

The production is primarily filming in Sydney, where it is expected to generate A$55 million (US$38 million) for the state of New South Wales, with Penang serving as part of its international setting.

Malaysia’s national film agency Finas said its chairman Hans Isaac visited the set on Sunday. Penang Global Tourism also welcomed Wahlberg and the crew, noting it was “delighted” that the production had chosen locations across the state.

Wahlberg, who will be in Penang for about two weeks, also posted a video after meeting a priest at a local church. He signed off with, “Stay prayed up, from Malaysia.”

His visit follows Florence Pugh’s appearance high above Kuala Lumpur in Marvel’s Thunderbolts, whose opening sequence featured her character leaping from Merdeka 118, the world’s second-tallest building.

The 2018 film Crazy Rich Asians also used Malaysian locations in Kuala Lumpur, Penang and Langkawi to create parts of its gilded Singaporean world.
