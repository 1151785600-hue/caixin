Gulf capital flowing into Asia as trade with Hong Kong rises 35%: Paul Chan

SCMP | FREE DEEP | 694 words | 2026-07-16

Capital from the Gulf is showing signs of flowing into Asia, Hong Kong’s finance chief has said, as bilateral trade between the region and the city rose by 35 per cent year on year in the first five months of 2026.

In his weekly blog on Sunday, Financial Secretary Paul Chan Mo-po said Hong Kong and the Middle East had deepened ties in the areas of the economy, trade, finance, innovation and technology, and culture over the past few years.

While trade between the Gulf states and Hong Kong rose by about 5 per cent last year, the figure increased by 35 per cent year on year in the first five months of 2026, he added.

In particular, Chan highlighted that bilateral trade between the city and the United Arab Emirates had surged by more than 52 per cent over the same period.

“In terms of capital flows, Gulf sovereign wealth funds previously invested mainly in American and European markets. However, of the tens of billions of US dollars they allocated globally last year, about 40 per cent flowed into Asia,” he said.

“This reflects a clear shift in their asset allocation, with a diversified asset allocation strategy gradually taking shape.”

Professor Terence Chong Tai-leung, executive director of the Chinese University of Hong Kong’s Lau Chor Tak Institute of Global Economics and Finance, said the US-Israel war on Iran and Middle East conflicts had prompted Gulf capital to diversify investment risks.

The economist said he believed the increase in bilateral trade between the Gulf states and Hong Kong was mainly due to rising energy prices.

Chong said the United States-Israel war on Iran could also have increased the extent to which Hong Kong re-exported mainland Chinese electronic products to the Middle East, in addition to boosting trade between Hong Kong and the region.

“The rise is mainly driven by the war and the prices. Looking ahead, the growth rate may not be significant unless Hong Kong and the Middle East grow very close, and products of small and medium-sized enterprises can be sold there,” he said.

Chong said that the Gulf states could just trade directly with the mainland, while Hong Kong had little demand for common Middle Eastern exports, such as oil products and spices.

He urged Hong Kong to attract more family offices and capital from the region to invest in financial products and companies.

He added that the city should step up its Islamic finance services, including rolling out relevant financial products, training dedicated personnel and facilitating smoother capital flows between the Middle East and Hong Kong.

In his blog, finance chief Chan also highlighted the I&T exchanges between Hong Kong and the Middle East, noting that LEAP, one of Saudi Arabia’s top technology conferences, was held in Asia for the first time at the Hong Kong Convention and Exhibition Centre from last Wednesday to Friday.

He said the LEAP East event attracted over 25,000 participants, more than half of whom had come from outside Hong Kong, and brought together leading firms, industry park operators and investors from over 30 countries and regions.

Chan added that investors attending the event managed US$6.5 trillion worth of assets.

Noting that Hong Kong would continue to host the conference for the next three years, Chan said staging world-class I&T events and related activities in the city provided a “valuable window” for young people to learn more about global developments.

This included applications of artificial intelligence (AI) in healthcare, finance, energy and industrial manufacturing, he said.

Ahead of the release of Diploma of Secondary Education exam results on Wednesday, Chan urged students to embrace AI and integrate it with their interests to explore such applications in society and develop new solutions to problems.

He also encouraged candidates who sat this year’s exams to remain diligent and curious in the age of rapid technological development.

Chan said the government was investing in AI resources as the technology was reshaping the competitiveness of various economies.

He added that the city was also increasing interaction with non-local markets to create opportunities for young people and the workforce amid the wave of new technology.
