Your Hong Kong weekend food guide for July 10-12

SCMP | FREE | 418 words | 2026-07-16

From a rooftop Italian newcomer in Tseung Kwan O to Jean-Pierre’s anniversary in Central and a Portuguese bakery opening on Lyndhurst Terrace, this week’s line-up mixes destination dining with a few well-earned treats – whether the mood calls for a leisurely lunch, a celebratory dinner or a sweet Sunday stop.

For dining recommendations outside this weekend, check out the 100 Top Tables Guide 2026.

Friday, July 10

Ponentino arrives in Tseung Kwan O as a new Italian dining destination with the pedigree of the hotel’s renowned Tsim Sha Tsui spot Sabatini, but offering a more relaxed take on the cuisine. On the rooftop of The Royal Garden Kowloon East, the new restaurant leans into elevated Italian classics, from Botan Ebi shrimp carpaccio with salmon roe, caviar, green apple and yuzu sorbet, to fig stracciatella, linguine with Spanish red prawn, and pan-fried toothfish with seasonal vegetables and Franciacorta cream sauce.

The menu is designed to move from lighter, brighter flavours to richer comfort dishes. Tasting menu, à la carte and semi-buffet options are all set against bay views and an airy, resort-style setting. Desserts such as home-made tiramisu and apple pie with vanilla gelato add a familiar, indulgent finish, making Ponentino feel like a destination for both leisurely lunches and decadent evening meals.

Where: 47/F, The Royal Garden Kowloon East, 3 Tong Tak Street, Tseung Kwan O

When: Daily, 12pm-12am

Price: Tasting menu HK$528 (lunch) to HK$788 (dinner). Semi-buffet lunch HK$358 (weekdays) to HK$398 (weekends and public holidays)

Saturday, July 11

Jean-Pierre in Central marks its first anniversary with a celebratory French bistro menu that leans into the restaurant’s Parisian mood, plush interiors and lively, late-night energy.

From starters of steak tartare and onion soup, through main courses of beef bourguignon and poulet de Simone, to desserts such as chocolate profiteroles and crêpes Suzette, the menu captures the old-school glamour that has made this one of Hong Kong’s most distinctive French dining rooms.

Where: 9 Bridges Street, Central

When: Through July

Sunday, July 12

Santa Nata is bringing freshly baked Portuguese egg tarts to Central with the opening of its first Hong Kong – and Asia – location, on Lyndhurst Terrace. The signature pastel de nata is joined by the Santa Nata brioche and pão de Deus (bread of God) – a sweet roll filled with coconut paste and finished with a layer of nata custard. Drinks include nata latte and nata milk tea.

Where: 25–27 Lyndhurst Terrace, Central

When: Daily, 10am-9pm
