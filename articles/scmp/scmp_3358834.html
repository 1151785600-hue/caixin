Your Hong Kong weekend food guide for July 3-5

SCMP | FREE | 380 words | 2026-07-08

From fifth-anniversary beef specials to a Japanese alfresco brunch and a harbour-view Sunday spread, this week’s guide is a showcase of the international flavours Hong Kong has to offer. Whether the mood is meat-led indulgence, polished brunching or a leisurely Sunday feast, there’s something here to suit every pace.

For more dining recommendations, check out our 2026 100 Top Tables Guide.

Friday, July 3

For diners looking to fulfil their meat cravings this Friday, Chef’s Cuts is marking its fifth anniversary with a summer campaign built around serious beef and bold flavours.

The highlight is a rotating series of dry-aged Black Angus rib-eyes, from Korean gochujang and black garlic to herb pesto and sake kasu, alongside a scallop set that runs through five sauces and five distinct flavour profiles.

Where: Available across all four Chef’s Cuts locations

When: Till September 30

Price: Ribeye HK$398; Scallop Odyssey set HK$188

Saturday, July 4

This Saturday, Zoku Restaurant & The Terrace at The Hari Hong Kong offers a polished brunch alternative with contemporary Japanese flavours, seasonal seafood and signature dishes like truffle yaki soba and A-4 Miyazaki Wagyu steak. Served in a stylish indoor-outdoor setting overlooking Wan Chai, the experience comes with free-flow drinks and an optional champagne and premium sake upgrade for a more leisurely weekend start.

Where: 2/F, The Hari Hong Kong, 330 Lockhart Road, Wan Chai

When: Saturdays and public holidays, 12pm-4.30pm

Price: From HK$628; champagne and sake package an additional HK$368

Sunday, July 5

The Orient Steakhouse’s new weekend brunch is built for slow Sunday dining, with harbour views, freshly cooked dishes and a generous spread that moves from bright Southeast Asian starters to steaks, seafood and a trio of shared desserts.

The meal includes three starters of pomelo rujak salad, tuna betel leaf and Hokkaido scallops. The choice of mains range from Indonesian-style three yellow-chicken and a banana leaf roast sambal sea bass to three available cuts of steak, accompanied by a side and desserts. There are also optional free-flow packages, from alcohol-free refreshment to bottomless Veuve Clicquot for a more celebratory finish.

Where: Shop OTE401, Level 4, Ocean Terminal, Harbour City, Tsim Sha Tsui

When: Saturdays, Sundays and public holidays, 11.30am-2.30pm

Price: HK$348; free-flow packages from HK$88
