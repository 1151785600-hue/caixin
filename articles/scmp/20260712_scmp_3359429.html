Hong Kong police arrest driver after high-speed chase ends in 5-vehicle crash

SCMP | FREE | 234 words | 2026-07-12

Police have arrested a man on suspicion of dangerous driving and trafficking dangerous drugs after he fled a stop and led officers on a high-speed chase across Hong Kong Island, ending in a five-vehicle crash that left one person injured.

The force said officers attempted to stop a suspicious car on Wah Fu Road near Waterfall Bay in Aberdeen at 4.57pm on Saturday, but the driver sped off, heading through the Aberdeen Tunnel towards Causeway Bay.

At about 5.35pm, the driver collided with a taxi, a truck, two cars and a police vehicle on the Canal Road Flyover near the Wong Nai Chung Gap Flyover, opposite Times Square.

A female taxi passenger sustained minor injuries and was taken to Ruttonjee Hospital in Wan Chai.

Police said the man was arrested on suspicion of dangerous driving and trafficking dangerous drugs.

Online footage shows the suspect abandoning his vehicle on the flyover and attempting to flee before being subdued by two officers. His T-shirt appeared torn, with a large hole at the back.

The Transport Department said some lanes on the Canal Road Flyover towards the Cross-Harbour Tunnel, near Leighton Road, were closed due to the incident.

All lanes on the Wong Nai Chung Gap Flyover bound for the tunnel, near the Aberdeen Tunnel exit, were also closed.

Motorists were advised to use Wong Nai Chung Road or Morrison Hill Road instead.
