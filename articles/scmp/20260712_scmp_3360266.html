World Cup 2026: Hong Kong pubs, restaurants enjoy net gains from big matches

SCMP | FREE DEEP | 665 words | 2026-07-12

Many bars, restaurants and shopping centres in Hong Kong have enjoyed brisk business thanks to the World Cup, with top nightlife district Lan Kwai Fong recording an up to 70 per cent jump in patronage for this year’s big matches.

Lan Kwai Fong Group chairman Allan Zeman told the South China Morning Post that he had joined football fans in the entertainment district in Central to catch the England-Norway game during the early hours of Sunday morning.

“It’s [been] a crazy night. It’ll only get crazier once we get into the finals,” Zeman said. “There are not many nights that get that full at 4am with both locals and tourists.

“Business is up 60 to 70 per cent,” he said, comparing important World Cup games involving big-name teams such as England, Brazil and Argentina to normal days.

But Zeman stopped short of sharing his expectations for the final, only expressing confidence that Lan Kwai Fong would be packed out for the occasion.

The World Cup has injected some much-needed buoyancy into business at the city’s pubs, bars and restaurants, which have grappled with the years-long consumer trend of Hongkongers heading to mainland China for cheaper options.

Patrons flocked to their venues of choice to watch the showdown between Norway and England, which saw the latter come out on top and be ushered into the semi-final.

A highlight in the run-up to the game was Norwegian player Erling Haaland, whose iconic internet persona and promotion of a Chinese herbal tea brand Wanglaoji, also known as Wong Lo Kat in Cantonese, have made him a sensation in China.

The high-profile match even saw budget airline Norwegian Air Shuttle replace their Instagram icon with the British Airways logo in a show of sportsmanship over England’s win.

But at Lin Heung Lau, a 100-year-old dim sum restaurant in Central screening the match, some fans expressed disappointment at being unable to see the match on the second floor’s gigantic monitor because of the crowds.

Others also complained about their inability to get tables for the event.

“Why did people on the second floor tell me I had to book, but the restaurant staff said there was no booking service?” football fan Amy Ng said.

Viral online footage even shows a queue of at least 100 people outside the restaurant around 4am.

Zeman, meanwhile, said many bars and restaurants were offering various World Cup experiences for patrons, including breakfast services for early-morning games.

“We’re just in the final stretch. In this time of the world, with the Iran war and everything else, you could not have a better attraction to bring the world together,” he said.

In Tseung Kwan O, Chi Lin Buddhist Primary School held a “Fun Day” for parents and students to watch the match in the morning, after ditching a previous plan to hold an overnight watch party, with families excited to see the game together.

The Norway-England match was far from the only crowd-pleaser of the day, with hundreds visiting the Citywalk 2 shopping centre in Tseun Wan at 9am to catch the showdown between Argentina and Switzerland.

Among the crowd of standing and sitting football fans was Mr Yip, who said he was excited to see his idol, Argentinian superstar Lionel Messi, crush Switzerland and take his team into the semi-finals.

The Dorsett hotel in Kai Tak even enjoyed an uptick in business thanks to the World Cup, with a spokesman saying the 373-room venue witnessed a significant increase in bookings for the semi-finals and the final matches.

“We expect a full house for the final night as fans want to watch the 3am match in comfort and crash right after,” he said.

The spokesman said the hotel expected the momentum generated by the World Cup would carry into July and August for the Hong Kong Football Festival and the Audi Football Summit featuring FC Bayern Munich vs Aston Villa FC.
