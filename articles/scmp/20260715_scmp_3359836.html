Mirror star Keung To slapped with 3-month driving ban after spate of offences

SCMP | FREE | 286 words | 2026-07-15

Hong Kong Cantopop star Keung To has been disqualified from driving for three months after accumulating 15 demerit points for various traffic offences last year.

Eastern Court on Wednesday revoked Keung’s probationary driving licence and ordered him to surrender the document within 14 days.

But a lawyer representing the 27-year-old artist, who was absent from the hearing for work-related reasons, said Keung had lost his licence and reported the matter to police.

Principal Magistrate David Cheung Chi-wai asked Keung to make a verbal declaration at the court’s registry if the defendant was unable to locate the document before the deadline.

Cheung also warned Keung that the disqualification order would bar him from driving all types of vehicles.

“The court will not tolerate any violation [of the order] and will sentence the defendant to immediate imprisonment,” the magistrate said.

A member of the popular boy band Mirror, Keung was fined a total of HK$2,200 (US$280) for various traffic offences after obtaining his probationary driving licence in February last year.

Keung pleaded guilty to careless driving after he rammed the front side of his car into railings on Caine Road at around 4am on November 28. He told police that he had lost control of the vehicle while reaching for his cap in the rear compartment.

The singer also confessed to running a red light at a junction in Kennedy Town in the early hours of December 23, as well as failing to display a probationary sign on his car while driving.

Under the Road Traffic (Driving-offence Points) Ordinance, any driver who incurs 15 or more demerit points within two years will be disqualified from driving for three to six months, unless a magistrate grants an exemption.
