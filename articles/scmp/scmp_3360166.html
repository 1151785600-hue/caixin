Friend sues ex-plastic surgeon for HK$12m over loans tied to manslaughter case

SCMP | FREE DEEP | 571 words | 2026-07-10

A former Hong Kong plastic surgeon has been sued for more than HK$12 million he allegedly owes to a friend who lent him the money to help him contest a manslaughter charge arising from a botched botox injection eight years ago.

Retiree Diana King Yuen-fung initiated the lawsuit in the High Court on Thursday demanding that Franklin Li Wang-pong immediately return the money he borrowed through 14 bank transfers between May 2023 and August 2024.

Li, 94, was implicated in a high-profile trial last year for gross negligence while performing a botox procedure on a 52-year-old banker at his clinic in Grand Centre, Tsim Sha Tsui, on November 11, 2018.

He was granted an absolute discharge in November after a High Court jury found him unfit to stand trial due to advanced dementia.

The panel nonetheless ruled that Li had committed all the acts alleged in the indictment, including his failure to properly assess the risks of administering sedatives to the patient and the concealment of critical information from police and hospital staff.

Prosecutors dropped 21 separate charges of misleading police and failing to maintain a register of dangerous drugs against Li earlier this year, citing his mental condition.

The High Court writ, seen by the South China Morning Post on Friday, said King became acquainted with Li in early 2023 and had met him and his wife for dinner on numerous occasions since then.

Li first told the plaintiff in April 2023 that he was facing criminal charges in relation to the botched procedure.

The former doctor said he did not wish to be jailed, but was having difficulty financing his legal team to defend him.

That month, King verbally agreed to send money to Li at his request from time to time on condition that he would repay the sums upon demand.

The plaintiff transferred amounts ranging from HK$33,660 to HK$3 million to Li’s bank accounts in 14 transactions between May 2, 2023, and August 9, 2024, totalling HK$12,170,280.01.

The court filing said King repeatedly asked Li to repay the loans from mid-2025, but the latter failed to do so.

Li allegedly tried to reassure his lender by claiming he had “a very rich son in the United States” named Warren H. Li who could repay the debt on his behalf.

“However, public records do not show there is such a person called ‘Warren H. Li’,” the writ said.

King’s lawyers asked the court to order Li to compensate her for losses and damages arising from his breach of the loan agreement.

The manslaughter trial heard that Li unlawfully killed Zoe Cheung Shuk-ling by giving her a mixture of sedatives and painkillers during the botox procedure, increasing the risk of breathing difficulties and cardiac arrest.

Cheung, who was then managing director of Swiss private bank Julius Baer in the city, fell into a coma and never regained consciousness. She was pronounced dead in hospital the next day.

Li, his wife, Veronica Li Lau Wei-chue, and their son, Dr Konrad Li Ming-sheng, attempted to resuscitate Cheung using a bag valve device before calling police.

The managers of Cheung’s estate previously sued the Li family and the ex-doctor’s clinic on grounds of negligence, breach of duty of care and causing “culpable delay in summoning emergency services to convey the deceased to hospital”.

The Medical Council removed Li from the general register of medical practitioners on February 4 this year due to his mental incapacity.
