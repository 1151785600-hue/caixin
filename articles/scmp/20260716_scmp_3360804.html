Short-haul tourist arrivals fall 15% amid strong Hong Kong dollar, fewer flights

SCMP | FREE | 440 words | 2026-07-16

Hong Kong’s short-haul visitor arrivals plunged by 15 per cent in June despite an overall growth in traffic, as higher fuel costs linked to the Middle East conflict reduced flight capacity and weaker regional currencies dampened travel demand, tourism authorities have said.

The Hong Kong Tourism Board said on Thursday that the city received 3.72 million visitors last month, up by 7 per cent from a year earlier, driven largely by a 10 per cent rise in mainland Chinese arrivals to 2.88 million.

But the number of non-mainland visitors fell by 4 per cent to 837,962, with the decline in short-haul travellers partially offset by a 16 per cent increase in long-haul arrivals to 277,034.

The board attributed the decline to elevated aviation fuel costs, which had prompted some airlines to cut capacity, as well as currency depreciation against the Hong Kong dollar in short-haul markets.

Fuel prices surged following the outbreak of the US-Israeli war on Iran, forcing carriers across Asia to raise fares, impose surcharges, suspend routes or trim capacity.

Malaysia-based budget carrier AirAsia cut 10 per cent of its capacity and suspended underperforming routes at the height of the fuel crisis, although it expects to fully restore capacity by the end of August.

While jet fuel prices have retreated from an April peak, they have edged up again in recent weeks.

The latest data from the International Air Transport Association showed the global average reached US$127.06 per barrel in the week ending July 10, up by 6.7 per cent from the previous week.

Hong Kong flag carrier Cathay Pacific Airways lowered fuel surcharges on most passenger flights from July 1 for the second time in less than two months.

The surcharge on flights between Hong Kong and South and Southeast Asia was reduced by 14.5 per cent, from HK$633 (US$80.60) to HK$541 per sector, while the charge for other regional routes fell from HK$339 to HK$290.

For the first half of the year, Hong Kong received 26.71 million visitors, an increase of 13 per cent from the same period in 2025.

Mainland arrivals rose by 16 per cent to 20.56 million, while the number of non-mainland travellers grew by 5 per cent to 6.16 million.

But short-haul arrivals fell by 2 per cent to 3.21 million over the six-month period, contrasting with a 19 per cent surge in long-haul visitors to 1.99 million.

The board said arrivals from France, Canada and Australia each recorded year-on-year growth of at least 20 per cent.

More than 100 large-scale meetings, incentive travel, conventions and exhibitions were held in Hong Kong in the first half, with overnight arrivals linked to the sector rising by 12 per cent year on year between January and May.
