Sam Altman signals OpenAI price war as rivalry with Anthropic, China heats up

SCMP | FREE DEEP | 549 words | 2026-07-15

OpenAI founder and CEO Sam Altman has signalled that the company was prepared to slash the price of its latest artificial intelligence models, as competition intensifies with US rival Anthropic and a growing wave of cheaper, rapidly advancing Chinese alternatives.

In a social media post on Tuesday, Altman noted that OpenAI’s flagship GPT-5.6 Sol was already “half the price” of Anthropic’s Claude Fable 5, adding that OpenAI would be “happy to deliver at one-quarter of the price”.

OpenAI released GPT-5.6 Sol last week, with pricing set at US$5 per million input tokens and US$30 per million output tokens. Fable 5, the consumer version of Anthropic’s powerful Mythos 5 model, is priced at US$10 per million input tokens and US$50 per million output tokens.

AI tokens serve as the fundamental units that models use to process and generate information, underpinning tasks ranging from chatbot conversations to image and film-quality video production.

Altman’s remarks come after Anthropic gained recognition this year among global enterprise customers and software developers, with its Claude Code emerging as a popular AI coding tool.

China has emerged as another source of pricing pressure after domestic AI developers spent much of this year driving down the cost of frontier models. While OpenAI lowered prices for its GPT-5.6 series compared with previous products, its flagship and mid-tier models remain more expensive than major rivals from China.

Zhipu AI’s GLM-5.2, for instance, charges about US$1.40 per million input tokens and US$4.40 per million output tokens, while DeepSeek’s standard rates for the V4-Pro model are US$0.44 per million input tokens and US$0.87 per million output.

ByteDance has also stepped up pricing incentives. On Tuesday, its Volcano Engine cloud unit announced a promotion offering enterprise users of its Trae coding platform a 50 per cent discount on bundled AI model usage through the end of 2026. The move is the latest by Chinese AI providers to lure enterprise customers with lower inference costs.

Alibaba, Tencent and MiniMax have all rolled out discounts or promotional offers for flagship models in recent months. Alibaba owns the South China Morning Post.

The wave of price cuts has coincided with rapid improvements in the capabilities of both US proprietary models and Chinese open-weight models, making cost an increasingly important factor for users choosing between competing AI services.

While proprietary models restrict users to paid, cloud-based access, open-weight alternatives allow users to download the code for free and deploy it on their own local hardware.

Rising AI token costs were “a real concern for most organisations”, sparking an uptick in interest in open-weight models among larger global enterprises, according to a UBS report published last month.

Chinese open-weight models “are reaching a critical point of intelligence performance” relative to proprietary models, according to a Goldman Sachs research report published last week.

Still, GPT-5.6 appears to excel in inference efficiency. According to independent AI benchmarking and insights platform Artificial Analysis, the model completed coding-agent tasks using roughly one-ninth of the output tokens consumed by DeepSeek V4 while achieving higher overall scores.
