Typhoon Bavi: flights and trains cancelled as China steps up emergency preparations

SCMP | FREE | 453 words | 2026-07-11

Authorities across eastern China have stepped up their emergency preparations and cancelled hundreds of flights as Typhoon Bavi, the ninth storm of the season, intensified overnight and barrelled towards the coast on Saturday.

The storm is forecast to make landfall between Taizhou in Zhejiang province and Fuding in Fujian province in the early hours of Sunday, packing winds of up to 45 metres per second (148 feet per second), according to the National Meteorological Centre (NMC).

In its 6am bulletin on Saturday, the NMC said Bavi had regained strength – reaching severe typhoon status at midnight. Its centre was located about 540km (340 miles) away from the Zhejiang-Fujian border.

Moving northwest at up to 35km per hour, the typhoon is expected to maintain its intensity before landfall, then turn north-northwestward and gradually weaken inland.

Emergency response teams have already been mobilised in the affected regions, including firefighters and crews to repair power grids.

The East China Sea Rescue Bureau put vessels and crews on standby. Fujian and Taizhou have also equipped and activated thousands of local volunteers who are on 24-hour standby to respond to the typhoon.

The meteorological agency has issued an orange typhoon warning, the second highest level in a four-tier system, alongside orange alerts for rainstorms and yellow alerts for severe weather.

Air travel has been severely disrupted. According to flight tracking data, 56 airports were projected to be affected from Friday to Monday.

All inbound and outbound flights at Taizhou Luqiao Airport were cancelled for Saturday, while cancellation rates at Wenzhou Longwan and Fuzhou Changle airports approached 100 per cent.

In Shanghai, both Pudong and Hongqiao airports are expected to see reduced capacity, with 387 flights – 300 at Pudong and 87 at Hongqiao – cancelled.

Rail services have also been curtailed.

In Zhejiang province alone, 29 high-speed trains and nine conventional trains were suspended on Saturday. Railway authorities have planned temporary stoppages on several lines.

Coastal regions have activated sweeping safety measures.

Fujian raised its typhoon emergency response to Level 2 on Friday afternoon, ordering all schools and training institutions to halt outdoor activities and construction work.

Also on Friday afternoon, Zhejiang has raised its response to Level 2, shutting down all scenic spots in Wenzhou, suspending 38 ferry routes and halting 48 water-related construction projects.

In Guangdong, 21 passenger ferries and 117 construction vessels have been taken out of service, with 108 workers evacuated from offshore wind platforms as of 2pm on Friday.

Shanghai issued a blue typhoon warning, the lowest level, at 10am on Friday, forecasting gales of force 8 to 9 – and up to 11 near the coast and high-rise buildings – between Saturday and Monday.

Meanwhile, the NMC warned of torrential rain and potential tornadoes in parts of northern China, including Beijing, Tianjin and Hebei between 8am Saturday and 8am Sunday.
