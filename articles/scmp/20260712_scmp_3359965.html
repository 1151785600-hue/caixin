Hong Kong corruption watchdog charges 3 over HK$1.3 million elderly care voucher fraud

SCMP | FREE | 324 words | 2026-07-12

Hong Kong’s anti-corruption watchdog has charged three people, including two private care home operators, over an alleged HK$1.3 million (US$166,700) fraud involving an elderly care voucher scheme.

The Independent Commission Against Corruption (ICAC) said on Thursday that the trio allegedly inflated service records under the Social Welfare Department’s Community Care Service Voucher Scheme for the Elderly, overstating carers’ service hours by more than 5,000 hours.

The scheme provides elderly residents with subsidies to purchase home- or centre-based care services from registered providers based on their needs.

Those charged are Lo Yuen-yee, 55, a registered nurse and director of Healthy Life Association Limited, Chan Yu-wai, 49, a registered occupational therapist and director and shareholder of Spark Plus Company Limited, and Mak Chui-shan, 58, a personal care service officer employed by both companies.

The ICAC said it had launched an investigation after a corruption complaint was referred by the department, alleging the trio used false records to obtain government subsidies.

At the time of the alleged offences, both Healthy Life Association and Spark Plus were registered service providers under the scheme and were required to submit attendance records to claim reimbursements.

Investigators found that between November 2023 and May 2025, the three defendants allegedly conspired with 10 carers deployed by Lo or Mak to falsify attendance records submitted by the two companies.

The alleged overstatement of more than 5,000 service hours involved over 130 voucher users and resulted in the companies receiving more than HK$1.3 million in subsidies, according to the investigation.

The trio were jointly charged on Wednesday with two counts of conspiracy to defraud. They have been released on bail and are due to appear at Eastern Court on Friday.

The prosecution will apply to transfer the case to the District Court for plea.

An ICAC spokesman said the commission would not tolerate the abuse of social welfare schemes for personal gain and had reviewed procedures under the voucher scheme, making recommendations to prevent similar incidents.
