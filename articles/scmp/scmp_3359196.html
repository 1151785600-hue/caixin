Hong Kong’s box office takings for first half of 2026 up 25% year on year

SCMP | FREE DEEP | 628 words | 2026-07-08

Hong Kong’s box office takings for the first half of 2026 rose by nearly a quarter year on year, with the comedy Night King emerging as the highest-grossing film over the six-month period.

According to figures from Hong Kong Box Office, 142 films premiered between January 1 and June 30, of which 17 were locally made. The movies generated HK$664 million (US$85 million), up from HK$531 million in the first six months of last year.

“The summer film season has just begun with many family-friendly offerings. The latter half of the year will also boast many high-quality domestic and international movies,” the industry body said on Thursday.

“It is hoped that the summer season and the second half of the year will sustain the strong momentum seen in the first half.”

Hong Kong Motion Picture Industry Association chairman Crucindo Hung Cho-sing noted that box office receipts specifically for local films rose by 40 per cent year on year.

“It really boils down to the quality of the films,” Hung said. “This goes to show that if there are good films, viewers will be there. It’s not impacted by the fluctuations in the economy so much as it’s an affordable form of entertainment.”

Hung also highlighted that the mainland Chinese film Dear You had grossed HK$12 million since it debuted in the city two weeks ago, which Hung said was an “exceptional” performance for a film from over the border.

The film has raked in 1.9 billion yuan (HK$2.19 billion) at the box office since its release on the mainland in May.

Looking ahead, Hung remains confident about box office takings for the rest of the year, predicting they could reach HK$1.5 billion.

Night King was the top earner for the first half of 2026, recording HK$118 million in receipts so far. The comedy was released on February 17, targeting the Chinese New Year holiday period, and is still screening locally.

The film, starring comedian Dayo Wong Tze-wah and singer-actress Sammi Cheng Sau-man, centres around a once-glamorous nightclub’s fight against a hostile takeover in 2012.

The film is rated category III, meaning it is only suitable for viewers aged 18 and over.

Wong also starred in the court drama A Guilty Conscience, which was the city’s highest-grossing film of 2023. It raked in HK$115 million and set a record for the most-watched film in a cinema, attracting an audience of 1.53 million.

But the record was broken the following year by the action film Twilight of the Warriors: Walled In.

The next highest earner for the first half of this year was the crime thriller Cold War 1994, which has earned HK$38 million since its debut on May 1.

The movie, which loosely follows the real-life story of a tycoon’s kidnapping, features superstars such as Chow Yun-fat, Louis Koo Tin-lok, Aaron Kwok Fu-shing and Fish Liew.

The comedy The Snowball on a Sunny Day, crime thriller We’re Nothing At All and sci-fi film Ciao UFO took the third to fifth places at the box office, with receipts ranging between HK$11 million and HK$16 million.

The best-performing international film over the same period was Avatar: Fire and Ash. The sci-fi epic, which screened between January 8 and April 22, earned HK$40 million.

The second best international earner was family-friendly animation The Super Mario Galaxy Movie, which grossed HK$37 million.

The top two were followed by the animated features Toy Story 5 and Hoppers, as well as drama The Devil Wears Prada 2, with box office takings that ranged from HK$27 million to HK$33 million.

Hong Kong recorded a 16 per cent year-on-year decrease in box office revenue in the first half of 2025. The percentage stayed the same for the full-year figure.
