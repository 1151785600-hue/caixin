Woman accused of money laundering in Wang Fuk Court fire case granted HK$300,000 bail

SCMP | FREE | 494 words | 2026-07-08

A Hong Kong court has granted bail to an assistant manager of the project consultant behind a HK$336 million (US$42.8 million) renovation project at the fire-ravaged Wang Fuk Court, pending a money-laundering trial.

Li Min, 33, secured bail at the High Court on Thursday after spending three weeks behind bars in connection with the city’s deadliest blaze in recent history. Her bail was set at HK$300,000, with her boyfriend also pledging HK$100,000 as surety.

Li was among seven individuals and two companies implicated in the first criminal case against those deemed responsible for the fire that broke out at Wang Fuk Court in Tai Po on November 26 last year.

Mr Justice William Tam Yiu-ho ordered Li to surrender her travel documents, reside at her boyfriend’s flat in Sha Tau Kok, report to police three times a week and avoid prosecution witnesses as part of her bail conditions.

Police and the Independent Commission Against Corruption (ICAC) levelled 25 counts against the nine accused, including manslaughter, conspiracy to defraud, money laundering, attempting to pervert the course of public justice and tax evasion.

Li faces a count of money laundering for allegedly using her personal bank accounts to handle more than HK$1.37 million in illegal proceeds on behalf of Will Power Architects from October 16, 2019, to November 30, 2025.

According to the ICAC, Will Power, the consultant behind Wang Fuk Court’s exterior overhaul, made false representations to residents to lure them into picking Prestige Construction and Engineering as the project’s main contractor.

Will Power also allegedly misled building authorities into believing Prestige’s false litigation history was true and that the consultant’s registered inspector had duly discharged his duties in supervising 86 building maintenance projects, including the works at Wang Fuk Court.

In the first hearing at West Kowloon Court on June 10, Chief Magistrate Victor So Wai-tak granted bail to Prestige director Hau Wa-kin, 54, and 41-year-old bank worker Chung So-fan, the wife of Will Power director Wong Hap-yin, 40.

The lower court dismissed the bail applications by Li, Prestige director Gordon Ho Kin-yip, 52, Will Power’s registered inspector Wilson Ng Yeuk, 44, and 60-year-old merchant Hung Kwok-wai, who is Wong’s friend and business partner.

Wong, who faces a total of 16 counts, did not apply for bail. Hung’s second bid for bail was rejected on June 18.

The case will be heard again at West Kowloon Court on September 2.

The 43-hour blaze was the deadliest in Hong Kong since 1948, killing 168 people and leaving nearly 5,000 homeless.

Prestige’s use of substandard scaffolding mesh and polyfoam boards during the works has been cited as contributing to the fire’s rapid spread across seven of the eight 31-storey towers at the estate.

The fire raised concerns over possible bid-rigging, as the estate’s renovation budget ballooned from a preliminary estimate of HK$152 million in September 2023 to HK$336 million in 2024, when Prestige’s offer – the most expensive option among 57 tenders – was adopted.
