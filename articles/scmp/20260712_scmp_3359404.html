2 wet lab buildings at Hong Kong Hetao innovation hub fully leased in first phase

SCMP | FREE DEEP | 637 words | 2026-07-12

The first phase of Hong Kong’s Hetao innovation hub, a key cross-border initiative under the Northern Metropolis megaproject, has been well received, with two of its wet laboratory buildings fully leased, its chairman has revealed.

Permanent Secretary for Innovation, Technology and Industry Kevin Choi Kit-ming, who also chairs the Hong Kong-Shenzhen Innovation and Technology Park in the Lok Ma Chau Loop, said on Saturday that more than half of the tenant companies were from mainland China.

“As of the end of May, some 90 enterprises had signed contracts with us. Virtually all floor space in the two wet laboratory buildings has been leased out,” he told a radio programme.

“The companies come from a wide and diverse range of backgrounds, with mainland China accounting for about 51 per cent, local enterprises making up around 35 per cent and overseas companies comprising roughly 14 per cent.”

The overseas enterprises included companies from South Korea, Switzerland and Finland, he added.

About 10 of the tenants were listed leading corporations, while around 70 per cent were start-ups.

“The new tenants showed a mix of both large and small enterprises, as we have some matching initiatives in place,” he said. “The goal is to have the leading companies guide the start-ups to help them grow gradually.”

Among the leading enterprises are Lenovo from Hong Kong, Xiamen Hithium Energy Storage Technology and UK-based AstraZeneca.

Choi added that, among the eight buildings in the first batch of phase one, two were wet laboratories and one was a residential complex for workers.

A wet lab works with liquids, chemicals and biological specimens.

The remaining five buildings, including the No 1 multipurpose building, which has been topped out and has leased 70 per cent of its space, are expected to be completed by the end of this year.

Choi said that nearly half of the leading corporations and start-ups were establishing a presence in Hong Kong for the first time.

As a cross-border collaboration platform between the mainland and Hong Kong, the loop offered unique advantages for foreign firms, he added.

“Many companies are eager to secure an early foothold here because of these advantages,” he said.

The innovation hub operates under the “one zone, two parks” vision.

The government earlier said it would set up dedicated boundary control facilities at a western bridge linking the two parks to serve people on a “white list”.

Only personnel directly involved in the development and operation of the Hong Kong park would be included, and they would still be required to undergo immigration and customs clearance, it said.

Choi said personnel from the two zones would be subject to different immigration and customs arrangements.

For example, Shenzhen personnel entering Hong Kong via the Lok Ma Chau Loop park would largely face no movement restrictions.

But he said Hong Kong personnel heading to the Shenzhen park would face more restrictions, such as only being allowed to visit certain parts of the park.

The Hong Kong park covers 87 hectares (215 acres), while the Shenzhen site spans 302 hectares.

The Hetao area plays a crucial role in advancing cooperation in the Greater Bay Area under the nation’s five-year plan covering 2026 to 2030.

The Hong Kong park will be organised into specialised clusters covering fields such as life and health technology, artificial intelligence and data science.

In an earlier wide-ranging interview with the South China Morning Post, Secretary for Innovation, Technology and Industry Sun Dong said Hong Kong and the mainland were expected to begin sharing cross-border data within the year, when more companies will start operating in the Hetao innovation hub.

He said a dedicated data facility was needed to ensure the relevant information was secure within the Hong Kong park, adding that various life science and artificial intelligence (AI) firms had already expressed interest in the policy.
