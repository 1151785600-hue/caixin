Senior Hong Kong policeman convicted of molesting clerk 3 times at headquarters

SCMP | FREE | 469 words | 2026-07-16

A senior Hong Kong police officer has been convicted of indecently assaulting a subordinate on three occasions at the force’s headquarters in Wan Chai, with one of the incidents taking place at a work party in full view of several colleagues.

Senior Superintendent Ian Chow Ngai-kong, 51, was found guilty by West Kowloon Magistrate Vivian Ho Wai-han on Thursday, after she dismissed the defendant’s testimony and ruled that his attempt to characterise their interactions as an affair was “nonsense”.

The victim, who was a government clerk, earlier told the court that Chow had molested her three times at police headquarters on June 19 and November 1, 2024.

She testified that the first incident that June involved Chow patting her buttocks outside his office while she was talking to his secretary.

At the work party in November of that year, Chow put his hand on the victim’s waist, before groping her buttocks for several minutes and using her hand to stroke his genitals.

The court heard that he then whispered to her that he wanted to spend the night with her.

Three female colleagues at the gathering testified that they witnessed the assaults. One of the three, a police officer, went over to the victim and pulled her away.

She went to another office before the defendant came in and continued molesting her for a third time.

At the trial, Chow denied the allegations and claimed they were having an affair that he had been concealing from his girlfriend of 20 years.

He claimed that their alleged relationship was limited to holding hands in his room and the occasional chats at work. He added that they never texted after hours or hung out in public because he wanted to keep it a secret.

But the magistrate dismissed his explanation as “unbelievable”.

“How could such interactions be considered an affair?” Ho said.

The defendant further claimed the victim had grown increasingly attached to him and had pressured him to break up with his girlfriend.

Chow argued that his physical contact with his subordinate was merely to comfort her, as he was worried she was upset over the end of their alleged affair.

Ho slammed Chow’s account as “completely incredible and illogical”, denouncing his testimony as “nonsense”.

The court also heard that the victim had tried to stop Chow’s advances by standing closer to another male officer, identified only as “William”, in the hope he would intervene.

Ho ruled that the victim and the eyewitnesses had given their credible testimonies, and convicted Chow of three counts of indecent assault.

She added that a custodial sentence was the only suitable punishment.

The maximum penalty for indecent assault is 10 years in prison but is capped at two years for cases heard before a magistrate.

Chow is scheduled to be sentenced on July 31.
