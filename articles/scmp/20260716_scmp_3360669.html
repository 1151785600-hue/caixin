2 prisoners found guilty of helping correctional officer attack fellow inmate

SCMP | FREE DEEP | 604 words | 2026-07-16

A Hong Kong court found two prisoners guilty of helping a correctional officer attack a fellow inmate who was left severely wounded after a stick was repeatedly thrust into his rectum.

The District Court on Wednesday convicted Bosco Lee, 21, and Brian Lam Shing-wai, 22, of wounding with intent to do grievous bodily harm for their involvement in a brutal assault on a prisoner at Pik Uk Prison in Sai Kung on December 26, 2023.

The main attacker, Tam Lik-chung, an ex-assistant officer with the Correctional Services Department, earlier pleaded guilty to the same offence.

But Lee and Lam, who were remanded and under Tam’s supervision at the time of the violence, denied taking part in it.

The assault happened after the victim – identified in court by his surname Chiu – sprinkled Tam with baby powder from behind during a bingo game in the prison’s activity room. One of the game’s rules was that the loser would get sprinkled.

After the game ended, Tam led the group to a staircase, where he told Chiu to bend down on his knees and take off his trousers.

According to the victim’s testimony, Tam said: “I will go gentle on you if you take off your pants. But if you do not, then I will pull out a stick.”

Chiu went down on all fours, but kept his underwear on. As Tam took the stick and repeatedly assaulted the victim with it for about 30 seconds, Chiu felt others controlling his arms.

The victim suffered immediate anal bleeding and dizziness. He was left bedridden until he called for help later that day due to suffering immense pain and being unable to urinate.

Chiu was sent to the hospital the next day for an emergency operation and received a stoma, which allows waste to leave the bowel and urinary systems.

Hospital doctors diagnosed him with rectal bleeding and found tears in the area.

Another inmate, Ho Lik-wun, who helped hold down the victim, earlier pleaded guilty to the same offence.

Chiu told the court that although he did not look up and see who held him down, he remembered Lee was standing by him before the assault.

The prison’s security camera captured inmate Lam entering the activity room and leaving with a stick before returning to the stairwell, where no camera was installed.

Judge Frankie Yiu Fun-che said it was “beyond a doubt” that Lam took the weapon and passed it to Tam. He also ruled that Lee took part in the assault, based on Chiu’s accounts.

Standing trial with inmates Lee and Lam were fellow inmate Li Yuxuan and a second prison officer, 56-year-old Kwok Siu-fai, who was Tam’s supervisor.

Prosecutors alleged Li was standing guard while the assault happened. But the judge found no evidence to show that he played a role in the assault or how exactly he was involved.

As for Kwok, he faced a count of misconduct in public office and testified that he had followed the department’s required procedures after Tam confessed the assault to him.

According to Kwok’s testimony, he found out the victim was sent to medical treatment after noticing Chiu’s absence the next morning. He said that upon questioning Tam, his subordinate confessed to assaulting the inmate.

Kwok said he requested Tam to report the case to the duty officer and did not ask for further details.

“Although there were substantial irregularities, the degree of gravity was not considered to be misconduct,” Yiu said in the verdict, while acquitting Kwok.

The convicted defendants will be sentenced on August 3.
