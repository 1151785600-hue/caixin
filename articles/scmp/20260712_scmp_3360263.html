Gulf capital flowing into Asia as Hong Kong-Middle East trade up 35%: Paul Chan

SCMP | FREE | 401 words | 2026-07-12

Capital from the Gulf region is showing signs of flowing into Asia, according to Hong Kong’s finance chief, as bilateral trade between the Middle East and the city rose 35 per cent in the first five months of the year.

In his weekly blog on Sunday, Financial Secretary Paul Chan Mo-po said Hong Kong and the Middle East had deepened ties in the areas of the economy, trade, finance, innovation and technology (I&T), and culture over the past few years.

While trade between the Gulf Arab States and Hong Kong rose about 5 per cent last year, the figure was up 35 per cent year on year in the first five months of this year, he added.

In particular, Chan highlighted that bilateral trade between the city and the United Arab Emirates soared by more than 52 per cent over the same period.

“In terms of capital flows, Gulf sovereign wealth funds previously invested mainly in American and European markets. However, of their tens of billions of US dollars allocated globally last year, about 40 per cent flowed into Asia,” he said.

“This reflects a clear shift in their asset allocation, with a diversified asset allocation strategy gradually taking shape.”

Chan also highlighted the I&T exchanges between Hong Kong and the Middle East, as the region’s flagship mega-event, LEAP East, was held in Asia for the first time at the Hong Kong Convention and Exhibition Centre from last Wednesday to Friday.

He said the event welcomed more than 25,000 participants, more than half of whom were from outside Hong Kong, and brought together leading firms, industry park companies and the investment community from over 30 countries and regions.

He added that investors attending the event managed a total of US$6.5 trillion in assets.

Noting that Hong Kong would continue to host the event over the next three years, Chan said organising world-class I&T events and related activities in the city served as a “valuable window” for young people to learn more about global developments in the sector. This included how artificial intelligence (AI) was adopted in healthcare, finance, energy and industrial manufacturing, he said.

Ahead of the release of results of the Hong Kong university entrance exam, the Diploma of Secondary Education examination on Wednesday, Chan urged students to embrace AI and integrate it with their interests to explore such applications in society and bring about new solutions to problems.
