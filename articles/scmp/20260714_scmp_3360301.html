Bangkok bar fire: 27 die as distraught relatives dread the worst

SCMP | FREE DEEP | 666 words | 2026-07-14

A blaze that tore through a packed Bangkok bar has killed at least 27 people and left nearly two dozen more in critical condition, with distraught friends and relatives on Monday dreading finding loved ones among the victims of the deadliest blaze to hit Thailand’s capital in over a decade.

Images shared on social media of the inferno, which erupted late on Sunday night, show flames shooting out from the entrance of the Na Ladprao pub, in Chatuchak district, as patrons fled, some with their clothes alight.

On the pavement outside, rescuers worked to resuscitate the injured, while dazed survivors staggered around the scene with bandages covering severe burns on their arms and legs.

The bar was crowded with hundreds of revellers when the fire took hold. Charred tables, bar stools, a drum kit and an electric guitar left on stage bore scorched witness to the speed with which the flames swept through the venue, trapping panicked people in the bathrooms, where many of the bodies were later found.

The bar’s Sunday-night headliners, a band called Tosakanth, lost their vocalist in the blaze, with two other members still missing on Monday morning.

“Breeze (our vocalist and my girlfriend) has passed away,” band leader Athipat ‘Ice’ Wijarn said in a social media post conveying the devastation.

Two other band members were later confirmed dead, while one called Din was found alive in hospital, although there was no immediate confirmation of his condition.

Earlier, Athipat told television reporters that smoke had suddenly poured from the ceiling before the power failed and fiery “explosions” ripped through the venue, leaving little time to react.

Prime Minister Anutin Charnvirakul said the fire might have started in a circuit breaker near the stage. “From what I have heard, most of the victims ran to the back of the building, probably looking for exits,” he told reporters.

Investigations began on Monday morning, with forensics teams combing through the wreckage.

A stunned young woman, speaking on Thai television, said she had been drinking inside the pub with her sister when the fire broke out.

“My sister was in the bathroom when I heard a scream ‘there’s fire’ so I ran out. All I could see was so much smoke everywhere, I couldn’t even breathe,” she told Amarin TV, which did not name her. “I don’t know where my sister is. I have reported a missing person. I’m praying that she’s OK.”

Thai police have set up an emergency desk for relatives to report people they fear to be missing in the blaze.

Deadliest fire in years

Attention on Monday turned to the cause of the fire, in a country where safety standards are often lax or ignored in the rush to pack venues with customers.

“Materials used in the building may have caused fire to spread fast … the smoke trapped in the building, we think people died of smoke inhalation,” Bangkok governor Chadchart Sittipun told a morning news programme on Channel 3.

“We need to check forensics, but when I was on site I didn’t really see the exit signs that visibly,” he said, adding the venue was licensed and had all the correct permits to operate.

A further 63 people injured in the fire have been hospitalised, according to the Bangkok Metropolitan Administration’s Department of Disaster Prevention and Mitigation.

Monday’s fire is the deadliest to strike Bangkok since 2009, when indoor pyrotechnics triggered an inferno on New Year’s Eve at the Santika Club in the city’s upscale Ekkamai district.

Sixty-seven people died in that blaze, shocking the nation and prompting an urgent reappraisal of safety standards in Thailand’s nightlife scene.

Yet venue operators across the country routinely flout those regulations, cramming bars and restaurants into areas where alcohol is allowed to be served.

Twenty-six people died at Mountain B club in Pattaya in 2022, in a blaze a court later ruled was caused by negligence. The club’s four owners were jailed.
