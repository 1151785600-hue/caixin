Your Hong Kong weekend drinks guide for July 3-5

SCMP | FREE DEEP | 590 words | 2026-07-08

There’s no shortage of vibrant summer flavours and soulful sounds to indulge in across Hong Kong this weekend. Local craft distillery Two Moons is releasing its new Ginger Gin, celebrating the launch with a heritage trail across three local bars. Elsewhere, you can escape the urban bustle at Take Five within Avalon for an intimate night of live jazz and complementary libations. Alternatively, you could ascend to Avoca on the 38th floor of Mondrian Hong Kong to preview the bar’s nostalgic new summer cocktail trio. Wherever you choose to unwind, a stellar weekend of drinking awaits.

Friday, July 3

Unwind with live jazz and refined sips at Take Five

Take Five, tucked discreetly within Avalon – the newly opened Western restaurant destination at H Queen’s – is an intimate live music bar and cocktail venue by Elite Concepts that offers an elegant sanctuary along Queen’s Road Central. The venue is at once named for the Dave Brubeck Quartet’s seminal jazz standard as well as the slang for a five-minute break, framing the lounge as a place for guests to step away from the urban bustle. Jazz guitarist Skip Moy is responsible for the venue’s rotating calendar of performances encompassing jazz, neoclassical and more.

Sandeep Kumar helms the bar, serving a concise beverage menu highlighted by five signature cocktails themed after famous jazz standards. The Green Dolphin Street features aged rum infused with pistachio shrub and topped with a light ricotta foam, while the Misty stars a cocoa-washed tequila layered with mango, calamansi and jalapeño.

Where: 2/F, H Queen’s, 80 Queen’s Road Central

When: From 4pm, music from 8.30pm-11pm

Saturday, July 4

Three Central bars celebrate Two Moons’ new Ginger Gin

In case you missed it, Two Moons Gin celebrated the launch of its Ginger Gin last week. Inspired by the humble yet irreplaceable staple of Cantonese culinary culture, the new spirit boasts a fiery, warming complexity. To mark the debut, Two Moons has partnered with three of Central’s finest artisanal establishments, Kinsman, 25:00 and Zzura, to serve two ginger-forward creations each that beautifully reframe local heritage through modern mixology.

At 25:00, the Chinese-style wedding cocktail Dragon & Phoenix structures the fiery gin with tart, local wampi fruit and lemon. Kinsman delivers Folk Medicine, a smoky Penicillin reimagining that combines the craft gin with sauce-aroma baijiu and red pickled ginger from the century-old Kowloon Soy Co. Meanwhile, Zzura celebrates the classic Hong Kong dessert with Tropical Wave, texturing the ginger gin using a velvety coconut milk-wash, fresh mango, pineapple and aromatic cloves.

Where and when:

25:00, 13/F, Luk Yu Building, 24-26 Stanley Street, Central; 6pm-2am

Kinsman, 65 Peel Street, Central; 6pm-2am

Zzura, 2/F, Amber Lodge, 23 Hollywood Road, Central; 5pm-2am

Sunday, July 5

Avoca unveils a sun-on-skin summer cocktail trio

Perched on the 38th floor of the Mondrian Hong Kong, Avoca is offering a limited-run cocktail trio for summer. Crafted by head bartender Rolf Flaminiano, the new cocktails reimagine everyday Hong Kong habits as sophisticated pours.

Cool down with Yik Lik Dor (Cantonese for Yakult), a playful nod to the much-loved probiotic drink that locals will know and love from childhood, elevated here with Iichiko Saiten shochu, passion fruit and Chinese black tea. Or sip the Mandarin Spritz – Aperol, pisco and mandarin soda – a sun-kissed pour that captures the languid ease of a Sunday morning ferry crossing. The seasonal menu will run until August 31.

Where: 38/F, Mondrian Hong Kong, 8A Hart Avenue, Tsim Sha Tsui

When: Till 1am
