Hong Kong police officer gets nearly 2 years for upskirt filming, gun grab

SCMP | FREE | 457 words | 2026-07-16

A Hong Kong police officer has been sentenced to nearly two years in jail for filming upskirt footage of a woman and attempting to grab a colleague’s handgun to kill himself after his arrest last year.

A deputy district judge handed down the sentence at West Kowloon Court on Monday after Constable Ko Chun-chung, 40, admitted the thrill of breaking the law drove him to commit the offence against the woman on January 15, 2025.

Ko, a married father of two with 15 years of service, had been under immense pressure since joining the force’s national security department, the court heard.

The officer had hoped that the temporary excitement of crime could help relieve stress, but felt ashamed and unable to face the consequences after being caught for the illegal act.

According to the prosecution Ko was off duty when he followed a woman onto an escalator at Tsim Sha Tsui MTR station at about 11.50am, after making a U-turn on the platform.

He used a Xiaomi smartphone to film between her legs, but was discovered when he accidentally struck the woman’s leg with the device.

Ko repeatedly apologised and begged for mercy as the woman seized his phone and called police.

When detained at Tsim Sha Tsui Police Station for questioning, Ko tried to grab the arresting officer’s revolver inside a reporting room at around 3pm. He and two officers were injured in the ensuing struggle.

Ko pleaded guilty last month to operating equipment with intent to record or observe intimate parts and to attempted possession of a firearm without a licence.

In mitigation, the defence highlighted Ko’s “reserved” personality and said the constable had struggled to process his pent-up emotions.

A government psychiatrist said Ko did not require further inpatient treatment for suicidal thoughts, while a clinical psychologist found the defendant had a low-to-medium risk of reoffending.

Deputy Judge Li Chi-ho accepted the offences were out of character, noting that Ko was highly regarded by his family, friends, colleagues and the force.

However, Li said the defendant’s attempt to snatch his colleague’s handgun could have put other officers and detainees in danger, particularly as he knew how to use the weapon.

While Ko deserved an immediate prison term for flouting the law, Li noted that the woman’s face was not captured in the footage, and that there was no evidence the video had been disseminated to others.

The court sentenced Ko to six weeks’ imprisonment for voyeurism and 20 months for the firearm offence before ordering a partially consecutive sentence, for a total of 20 months and four weeks.

Having been in custody since January 17 last year, Ko is expected to be eligible for early release soon.
