Hong Kong police arrest 4, seize drugs after man dies following dispute at flat

SCMP | FREE | 468 words | 2026-07-08

Hong Kong police have launched an investigation into the death of a man and arrested four people following an alleged attack in a Mong Kok flat, where drugs were also found.

A police spokesman said on Wednesday that officers received a call at about 2.50pm on Wednesday from a man who reported that his friend, 45, was attacking another acquaintance, 58, with scissors inside a flat at 22 Tong Mi Road.

Officers arrived to find the 45-year-old unconscious. He was taken to Kwong Wah Hospital in Yau Ma Tei, where he was later pronounced dead.

Initial examinations showed the man had no wounds consistent with scissors or significant bleeding but had minor injuries to his head, arms and legs.

The case was preliminarily classified as “assault occasioning actual bodily harm” before being upgraded to “murder”, according to a source.

In an update on Thursday, police said the case also involved “trafficking and consumption of dangerous drugs” and recorded the victim as “dead on arrival”.

The force also arrested another two men on Thursday.

The source said that the 35-year-old caller was the tenant of the flat and had invited the other two to his home earlier in the day for a “chemfun” sex party – a gathering involving drug-fuelled sexual intercourse between men.

“During the party, the deceased suddenly attacked the caller and another arrestee,” the source said.

“Three of the arrestees then got dressed and left the flat. The deceased was still conscious at that moment.”

Police found the 45-year-old naked in the living room, unconscious and barely breathing.

Officers also discovered a cache of suspected drugs during a search of the flat.

The first two arrests – of the 35-year-old tenant and a 58-year-old party guest – were made on Wednesday.

The tenant was apprehended on suspicion of trafficking dangerous drugs, while the 58-year-old was arrested for allegedly consuming dangerous drugs.

Police said two more men were held early on Thursday in connection with the case: a 53-year-old in a flat on Farm Road in Kowloon City and a 48-year-old in Chin Wah House, Lok Wah South Estate in Ngau Tau Kok.

Both were arrested on suspicion of consuming dangerous drugs.

The source said the deceased had met the four suspects through the gay dating app Grindr.

“The deceased was close to the caller, but not the others,” the source added.

When police first arrived at the scene in response to the call, only the caller and the deceased were inside the flat. The 58-year-old man later returned to assist with investigations, the source said.

Officers found multiple types of drugs, including ketamine, methamphetamine, cannabis, Viagra and Ecstasy in the flat.

Investigations into the cause of death are ongoing. The case has been handed to the Mong Kok district criminal investigation team.
