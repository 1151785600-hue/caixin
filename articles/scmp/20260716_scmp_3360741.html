Mainland China funds increase stakes in Hong Kong biotech amid surge of licensing deals

SCMP | FREE | 499 words | 2026-07-16

A growing number of mainland Chinese institutional fund managers have built up stakes in Hong Kong-listed biotechnology stocks over the past month, drawn by attractive valuations amid a surge in cross-border licensing deals in the sector.

China’s largest mutual fund manager, E Fund Management, has taken a 7 per cent stake in gene-editing biotechnology firm Biocytogen Pharmaceuticals, buying additional 91,500 shares in the Beijing-based firm at an average price of HK$46.90 each on July 2, according to the Hong Kong stock exchange website.

Fullgoal Fund Management also increased its stake in Biocytogen from 6.7 per cent to 7 per cent, acquiring shares at an average price of HK$48.2 on July 7. Biocytogen’s stock has surged about 44 per cent over the past month.

“We believe the sector’s valuation is attractive,” wrote Linda Shu, head of China healthcare research at HSBC, in a note on July 10.

Hong Kong-listed pharmaceutical stocks are trading at valuations considered relatively cheap by historical standards, Shu said, adding that multiple catalysts in the sector lay ahead in the second half of the year, such as a growing number of out-licensing deals.

The Hang Seng Innovative Drug Index, which tracks some of China’s most innovative pharmaceutical and biotechnology companies, including Innovent Biologics and Akeso, has risen by about 12.62 per cent over the past month.

The mainland-Hong Kong Stock Connect allows onshore investors to buy and sell shares listed on the city’s bourse and allows offshore investors to trade on mainland exchanges.

Government-backed China Universal Asset Management, which manages about 1 trillion yuan (US$147.72 billion) in assets, has been actively trading its holdings in novel drug developer CSPC Pharmaceutical Group since early July, increasing its holdings of the drug maker’s issued voting shares from 5 per cent to 5.05 per cent on July 10.

The fund has also boosted its stake in biopharmaceutical firm 3SBio’s issued voting shares to 6.9 per cent as of July 10 since late last year.

CSPC Pharmaceutical Group signed a new collaboration worth up to US$1.77 billion with pharmaceutical giant AstraZeneca on July 2 to jointly research and develop small nucleic acid drug candidates.

The capital flows come amid moderate southbound activity. The average daily turnover by value in southbound trading via Stock Connect, including stocks and ETFs, slipped 2.7 per cent to HK$130 billion (US$16.58 billion) in June compared with a month earlier, the Hong Kong Exchanges and Clearing website showed.

China’s biotechnology sector recorded multiple milestones in out-licensing deals in the first half, “which indicates Chinese innovation is being rapidly integrated into the global ecosystem”, Shu said.

Analysts also attributed undervaluation in the sector to recent US investment curbs.

The US Biosecure Act passed last year had cast a shadow over the sector, said Fabiana Fedeli, chief investment officer for equities, multi‑asset and sustainability at M&G, in a recent interview.

“Some of them actually are coming out with even more attractive valuations” as high-quality players would likely survive through partnerships with international counterparts, she said.
