China fines 4 internet celebrities US$1.9m over tax evasion involving e-commerce sales

SCMP | FREE | 410 words | 2026-07-11

Chinese authorities have fined four internet celebrities 13.3 million yuan (US$1.9 million) for tax evasion after they racked up millions of followers but paid nearly zero tax, Xinhua said on Friday.

The penalties, including fines and late-payment fees, were paid in addition to the outstanding taxes, the state news agency reported.

“The streamers involved have high popularity and large sales volume in their live broadcast spaces and often receive donations from fans, but the tax declarations and payments of these streamers were very low,” the report said.

These were among seven cases disclosed by tax authorities in Shandong, Sichuan and Zhejiang provinces, Xinhua said. The other three cases involved popular online stores, one of which had an annual store revenues of more than 100 million yuan.

Although computer systems were making it easier for Chinese tax bureaus to log workplace payroll information, the earnings of internet streamers and their stores remained more elusive, said Chen Zhiwu, chair professor of finance at the University of Hong Kong.

Celebrities usually open or endorse the stores, which include e-commerce shops.

In 2022, three government departments began requiring internet platforms to file regular reports on live-streamers, including their income details. Under previous guidelines, regulators would just advise people to “pay taxes properly”.

“Officials suddenly realised some internet celebrities haven’t paid taxes,” Chen said, adding that local governments were short of money because tax revenue derived from land sales was drying up.

Government finances have come under pressure with revenue declining amid an economic slowdown and a prolonged property crisis.

Among the four new cases, tax authorities found that Xu Jingwan, a live-streaming e-commerce host with a following of more than 6 million people, paid little personal income tax and paid no taxes on two online stores for which she had responsibility, Xinhua said.

Live-streamer Guo Xiaofeng, with more than 2.4 million fans and 1 million orders for a “shop window” account, paid “only” 13,500 yuan in taxes over four years, the report said.

Wang Yifan, with a following of about 1 million, was found to have paid slightly more than 2,000 yuan in personal income tax over four years, Xinhua added, while Chen Xu, another user with about 1 million fans, declared just a few hundred yuan in personal income tax in 2023.

In other cases discovered by local tax authorities, internet stores had hidden income by deregistering. Xinhua said a platform investigated in Shandong province had cancelled its business registration to conceal 5.4 million yuan in unpaid taxes.
