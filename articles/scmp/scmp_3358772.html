Fosun breaks ground at Shaw Brothers Movietown residential project

SCMP | FREE | 442 words | 2026-07-08

China’s Fosun Group began construction of its first residential project in Hong Kong, encapsulating the recovery of the city’s property sector as it transforms the Shaw Brothers’ Movietown studio complex in Clear Water Bay.

At the groundbreaking ceremony, Guo Guangchang, chairman of Fosun International, described the 12-year-old project as a vote of confidence in Hong Kong’s development, with the move taking forward the birthplace of Chinese kung fu films – once dubbed Asia’s Hollywood.

“We must always grit our teeth and pull through during difficult times,” Guo said. “I am confident that Hong Kong’s future will only get better and better.”

Fosun Group, the parent of Hong Kong-listed Fosun International, bought part of the complex in 2014. It will invest over HK$10 billion (US$1.28 billion) to build about 800 luxury units in 40 blocks and an additional 200,000 square feet for hotel rooms and commercial space, according to Leo Liu Yani, chairman of the group’s subsidiary, Forte Industry Development Group (Hong Kong Branch).

Fosun said it could launch the sale of units by the end of 2027 at the earliest and aimed to complete the entire project in 2030.

As part of the agreement, the developer was asked to preserve and maintain the Shaw Administrative Building, the adjacent film store, and the dubbing studios in situ.

Other property tycoons, such as Far East Group chairman David Chiu Tat-cheong and Lan Kwai Fong chairman Allan Zeman, attended the ceremony. The Ng family, which controls Sino Group, also sent two flower baskets to the ceremony.

Hong Kong’s home prices have rebounded 13 per cent as of May after hitting a trough in March 2025. However, they were still 19 per cent below the historic high seen in September 2021.

After buying the studio via two deals in 2014 and 2016 for a total of nearly HK$2.5 billion, Fosun paid a HK$2.18 billion land premium to the Hong Kong government in 2024, down by nearly 80 per cent from the amount in 2022, taking advantage of the market slump.

Guo, making a rare public appearance, said he would buy a unit in the Clear Water Bay project and spend the next 60 years of his life here and invite his entrepreneur friends to join him for walks and leisure.

Fosun has made large disposals of its non-core businesses in recent years amid a debt crisis and China’s crackdown on domestic developers.

Once dubbed the Chinese Hutchison, Fosun went on a global spending spree as it bought into a wide range of businesses from pharmaceuticals to tourism across more than 30 countries.

Fosun International’s market capitalisation also slumped from its peak of HK$230 billion in 2021 to HK$32.8 billion as of Monday.
