China seen using new tool to finance hi-tech advances amid slower economic growth

SCMP | FREE | 449 words | 2026-07-14

China could accelerate the deployment of an 800 billion yuan (US$117.94 billion) new financial instrument to support hi-tech development and help mitigate the forecast slowdown in quarterly economic growth, investment bank Goldman Sachs says.

Officials would use the quasi-fiscal New Policy-Based Financial Instrument, which was rolled out to fund strategic investments through policy banks outside traditional commercial bank lending channels, Goldman Sachs said.

“China’s slowing second-quarter growth has … raised market attention on a potentially faster implementation of the New Policy-Based Financial Instrument that was already planned for this year,” the New York-based bank said in a report on Sunday.

“The ongoing US-China technology race calls for larger and more durable funding support for hi-tech industries over the medium to long term,” said the report, written by China economist Wang Lisheng.

“Funding for China’s hi-tech manufacturing sector, including AI supply chains, remains largely government-led, as policymakers seek to narrow the gap in hyperscaler capex between the US and China,” Wang added.

With official data scheduled to be released on Wednesday, some economists expect that China’s economic growth slowed in the second quarter after a 5 per cent increase in the first. Weak domestic demand and geopolitical uncertainties have weighed on the world’s second-largest economy.

The use of the new financial instrument would meet the “dual objective” of supporting hi-tech investments while limiting local government debt risks, Goldman Sachs said. Traditionally, the financial tool offered “low-cost, long-term” funding with a goal of resolving insufficient equity capital, the report said.

Following years of trade and knowledge transfer restrictions by the US, China is aiming to become more tech self-sufficient. To that end, it has pledged to achieve a “safe and reliable supply” of bedrock artificial intelligence technologies by next year.

The National Development and Reform Commission announced the New Policy-Based Financial Instrument in September, worth 500 billion yuan at the time, to replenish project capital.

It had already been used to support AI, the low-altitude economy, green energy, agriculture and industrial estates, Goldman Sachs said.

Meanwhile, if the 800 billion yuan for this year emerges in the current quarter as planned, Goldman Sachs estimated a “baseline” gross domestic product (GDP) effect of 0.5 percentage points through early 2027.

The funding vehicle would “deliver a larger investment and growth multiplier” than outlays by older schemes such as local government financing vehicles, Goldman Sachs said. Those vehicles refer to state-owned companies often set up to raise capital for infrastructure.

Funding from local government financing vehicles was “likely to remain under pressure” in light of the 2024-28 debt resolution programme and China’s property market downturn, the investment bank added.
