Hong Kong ministers get 1.3% pay rise after civil servants secure 2% salary bump

SCMP | FREE | 464 words | 2026-07-08

Hong Kong’s top ministers and other political appointees have received a 1.3 per cent pay rise, authorities have confirmed, after the government announced in June that civil servants would get a 2 per cent salary bump.

In a reply to the South China Morning Post, the Constitutional and Mainland Affairs Bureau said on Friday that the decision was based on recommendations by an independent commission tasked with reviewing the salaries of politically appointed officials, lawmakers and the Executive Council.

“According to the mechanism approved by the Legislative Council Finance Committee, the pay of politically appointed officials is adjusted annually in line with the average annual change in the C category of the consumer price index,” it said.

The category covers about 10 per cent of Hong Kong households with higher average monthly expenditures.

“Under the annual adjustment mechanism, the cash pay of politically appointed officials at all levels will increase by 1.3 per cent from July 1, 2026,” the bureau said.

The increase aligns with inflation levels affecting those in category C. The figure rose by 1.3 per cent over a 12-month period ending in May.

Last year, the government introduced a pay freeze for the chief executive, politically appointed officials, non-official Exco members, all civil servants, lawmakers and other posts.

The decision was part of efforts to contain expenditure and tackle the city’s deficit.

The bureau did not provide the exact figures for the new salaries.

The SCMP calculated that the monthly salary of the chief secretary for administration, the city’s No 2 official, had risen to HK$422,755 (US$53,900). The figure is based on his previous salary of HK$417,330, which was cited in a letter to lawmakers in 2024.

For the financial secretary, the monthly salary will have increased from HK$403,215 to HK$408,457, while the pay for the secretary for justice will have risen from HK$389,580 to HK$394,645.

The three deputy secretaries of departments were previously paid HK$382,990 and now earn HK$387,969, while 15 bureau chiefs were paid HK$376,405 and now HK$381,298.

The pay rise for politically appointed officials follows Legco’s approval of a flat 2 per cent pay rise for civil servants this year.

Several lawmakers had criticised the increase, which took effect retroactively from April, saying it fell short of the pay trend survey’s recommendation and demands from unions.

The current pay mechanism for politically appointed officials was accepted by then-chief executive Carrie Lam Cheng Yuet-ngor in May 2022, based on a recommendation by the independent commission.

Aside from 2025, a pay freeze was also implemented for politically appointed officials in 2020 and 2021 at the height of the Covid-19 pandemic.

Between 2022 and 2024, the pay increase ranged from 1.8 per cent to 4.1 per cent.

With the latest adjustment, Hong Kong ministers and other political appointees have seen their salaries increase by around 27.8 per cent cumulatively over the last decade.
