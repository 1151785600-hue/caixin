Osaka-led industry group eyes China mission as corporate Japan navigates trade tensions

SCMP | FREE | 451 words | 2026-07-15

A Japanese business organisation with 1,300 members is planning to send a delegation to China for meetings that could bolster economic relations between the Asian economic powerhouses following a diplomatic dispute in November.

The Osaka-based Kansai Economic Federation intends to send its delegation to Beijing and Xian, Shaanxi province, from October 18-23, the group said in a statement to the South China Morning Post on Wednesday. It said the delegation expected to meet with Chinese government officials and other organisations.

Federation planners were awaiting confirmation from the China Council for the Promotion of International Trade (CCPIT), a semi-governmental agency in Beijing, according to the statement.

Masayoshi Matsumoto, the federation chairman, and Shingo Torii, chairman of the Osaka Chamber of Commerce and Industry, will lead the delegation. A 2024 entourage sent to China had about 80 members, and this year’s total is still being determined.

Tensions between China and Japan have run high since November, when Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi suggested that Tokyo could deploy military forces in the event of a conflict in the Taiwan Strait.

Beijing sees Taiwan as part of China to be reunited by force if necessary. Most countries, including the United States, do not recognise Taiwan as an independent state, but Washington is opposed to any attempt to take the self-governed island by force and is committed to supplying it with weapons.

Beijing responded to the prime minister’s comments by issuing travel warnings urging Chinese tourists to avoid Japan, halting seafood imports and banning exports of dual-use items to entities supporting the Japanese military.

Japan’s Kansai region, anchored by the cities of Osaka and Kyoto, is historically known for a “pragmatic merchant spirit”, and the Kansai-focused federation has previously advocated a “necessity” for closer economic relations with China, said Yoichiro Sato, dean of the College of Asia-Pacific Studies at Ritsumeikan Asia-Pacific University in Japan.

“Amid the high political tension between Japan and China, the upcoming visit could ease the bilateral political tension,” Sato said.

Trade between Japan and China, Asia’s two largest economies, reached US$322 billion last year. Some of the 80-year-old federation’s member companies do business in China. The group counts “international exchange and interaction” in the Asia-Pacific among its missions.

“The purpose of the visit is to reaffirm the strong ties between the Kansai business community and China and create an opportunity to further develop bilateral relations towards the future,” the statement said.

A federation trip to China would follow Tuesday’s meeting in Beijing between an official with the CCPIT and a counterpart from the Tokyo-backed Japan External Trade Organisation.

They discussed economic exchanges and industrial chain cooperation, according to the council.
